Tourism In Mauritania

Tourism in Mauritania can be a unique experience for investors, as well as for those seeking to discover the secrets of the Mauritanian desert in its ancient cities, which in the past were a major milestone on the medieval caravan route.

Mauritania, which is the meeting point of the Arab world and sub-Saharan Africa, is distinguished by its exceptional cultural heritage and diversity of landscapes; its vast desert that offers great opportunities for sports adventures, hunting and land, its mountainous terrain, picturesque beaches, parks and nature reserves, as well as the valleys and oases scattered throughout the Mauritanian desert La Ethnic diversity of the country is also a factor of wealth, as authentic Arab values embrace African traditions in a unique harmony. It can be said that the country has enormous tourism skills that offer promising investment opportunities such as: archaeological cities classified as human heritage by UNESCO (Chinguetti, Wadan, Tichet and Welatta), which attend annual festivals that have become a pilgrimage for a large number of tourists, and palm oasis in the states of Adrar, Takant and Adaba, in addition to a clean Atlantic coast,

are distinguished by their pleasant climate, their landscapes picturesque and their nature reserves (Cape-Vert, Argin basin and Diaoling), and the argin basin includes an archipelago of islands on the Atlantic coast (102 islands) on an area of 12 thousand square kilometers, and it is a favorite place for millions Birds and Migratory Animals Marine Blue It was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1989.

BANC DARGUN:

A Enesco World Heritage Site since 1989 and classified as a wetland of international importance at the Ramset Convention, the Banc d'Arguin covers an area of 12,000 km2 (including 6,300 maritime domains) on either side of the 20th parallel. There are beautiful landscapes (sandbanks, dunes, mudflats), a multitude of animal species (more than 280) and sea grass beds. It is a reserve that is home to many migratory birds from France, Holland or even Siberia: seagulls, cormorants, gulls, ash herons...

Wadan

Outdone, or the city at two wades, is an ancient settlement dating back to 1140. In the following centuries, the city flourished with Timbuktu, and was known for its palm groves, mosques and library. In the 17th century, the city was visited by the Portuguese, who established commercial links there at the time when the city reached its apogee. The decline, however, came as European maritime trade developed. UNESCO classifies the site as part of the World Heritage Site. Perched on steep rocks, the city evokes the glorious past of the Sahara. Inside the city, sites such as the old mosque, the palm plantation, the old town, the Richat structure, the meteorite studied by Theodore Monod, El Beyedh or the fort of El Ghallaouya.

Welata

Oualata, located 90 km north of the city of Nema, is an old town known for its wall decorations. The houses are painted with different motifs. The city developed in the 11th century. It was a caravan town like Ouadane, taking advantage of the trade with Timbuktu to develop.

Chinguetti

The town of Chinguetti is located 86 km from Atar, 128 km from Ouadane. Considered the 7th holy city of Islam, Chinguetti was a religious center and known for these Koranic schools. The city also played an important commercial role during the era of camel trade. The city's influence extended beyond the borders of present-day Mauritania, and schools were known as far east. Mauritania was even known at one time as the "BiladChenguetti", which means the land of Chenguetti, and had its heyday during the 17th and 18th centuries. In 1980, the city was attacked by the Polisario Front, which led Mauritania to withdraw from the conflict. Today, the old part to the south of the city falls into disrepair and is buried under the dunes, but continues to offer an impressive sight for the visitor. Old buildings, such as the 13th century mosque and minaret, still survive in the old town today. The city library contains hundreds of medieval manuscripts, detailing among other commercial transactions, Koranic laws and scientific observations.

Tichit

Tichitt is a remote but historic town, located 230 km east of Tidjikdja and 490 km from Nema. The history of the city dates back to the 12th century also taking advantage of trade in the Sahara to develop. The city is known for its 700-year-old library and mosque. Glass from Venice was found in the form of pearls, a witness to the medieval trade. Medieval commercial facilities are classified as part of the World Heritage Of Humanity.

Terjit

The oasis of Terjit is 50 km from Atar (1 hour drive) towards Akjoujt. An unsuspected paradise: in the shade of a fault in the plateau, water oozes along the rock walls to create natural reservoirs on the ground. The water is fresh and excellent to drink. An extraordinary site worth the trip.
