Agriculture in Mauritania:

The Islamic Republic of Mauritania, located in northwestern Africa between the 15th and 27th parallel north, is bounded to the west by the Atlantic Ocean and stretches over a 600 km coastline. The country covers an area of 1,025,520 km2 and more than half of the northern part of the national territory is desert and sparsely populated. The Sahelian zone extends from west to east on a 200 km strip that crosses the south of the country. In the centre and north, the terrain is made up of mountain ranges, such as those of the Adrar and Tagant, which peak from 400 to 800 meters. With the exception of the alluvial plain of the Senegal River to the south, called Chemama, the rest of the country consists, in large part, of dune alignments that, when it rains, are covered with pastures and where rain-fed crops are practised. Arable land accounts for less than 1 per cent of the land and the area under cultivation varies greatly according to rainfall: in 2002, it reached 500,000 ha

Agro-Ecological Zones Mauritania's :

  • •The arid or Saharan zone:

    with annual rainfall of less than 150 mm. It covers 80 per cent of the country's surface and includes the Wilayas of Tiris Zemmour, Adrar, Tagant, as well as the northern parts of the two Hodhs, Brakna and Trarza.

  • •The Sahelian zone:

    includes the 3 wilayas of southeastern Mauritania: the Assaba and the two Hodhs (with the exception of the 3 Moughataa included in the arid zone), the Moughataa of northern Brakna, Gorgol, Trarza and OuldYengé. The rains are irregular and vary between 150 and 500 mm per year. A summer rainy season and a dry winter season allow the practice of rain-fed crops (sorghum, millet, maize). In the eastern part, livestock farming is predominant compared to rainfed crops: traditional cereals.

  • •The area of the Senegal River Valley:

    although it covers only 2 per cent of the country's total area, this area has water resources and vegetation for the development of agro-sylvo-pastoral activities. It corresponds to the south of the 4 wilayas of the Senegal River valley (Trarza, Brakna, Gorgol, Guidimakha) where anthropogenic and animal pressures on resources are increasing and lead to processes of degradation of the natural environment already affected by drought.

  • • The area of the Atlantic:

    façade is spread over a strip of 800 km by 50 km wide between Nouadhibou and NDiago (Wilaya Trarza, Moughataa Keur Macène). The presence of the country's two main cities (Nouakchott and Nouadhibou) makes this area one of the most important in terms of commercial and industrial development.
