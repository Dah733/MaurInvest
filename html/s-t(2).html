Tourism Recovery In Mauritania 2017/ 2018:

The resumption of tourism by weekly charter flight Paris-Atar, after a delay of suspension, was launched on December 23, 2017. The last plane of the 12 flights of this tourist season landed at Atar International Airport last Saturday, March 24, 2018. In total, some 1,470 tourists have travelled various routes in Mauritania, almost all of them in Adrar. These figures, and those that will follow, are collected by Adrar.Info. The website of the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Tourism (http://www.commerce.gov.mr/) has been suspended since 26 May 2017; The one of the National Tourist Board ONT (http://www.tourisme-mauritanie.mr/office/index.php/fr/) is a frozen wall, advertising, which does not include statistical or current data on tourism. The direct financial benefits collected in large part, by the receivers. That is, the societies or people who welcome and take care ofaccommodation, food and transport of tourists to Mauritania. These receivers rent vehicles, camels, sometimes hostels and pay guides, cooks and camels. Some dividends, from the pocket money of some tourists, sometimes also fall into the hands of the managers of traditional libraries (Chinguetti, Ouadane..), traditional craftsmen and poor sand vendors, historic cities, oases and encampments. The overall results of this season are encouraging. By referling to the exchange rate of Mauritania's central bank on Tuesday, March 27, 2018, the Euro is worth 43, 65 (new currency).

However, charter tourism alone was, this year, a great relief to the national economy, which is facing enormous difficulties in cash flow, management and project prioritizing. Shouldn't this awaken, in the public authorities, the growing interest of the contribution of tourism to the country?

On the Mauritanian side, the rulers seem more concerned about the second order, especially the political-political war to stay in power beyond 2019.
